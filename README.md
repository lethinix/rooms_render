# 2110

a thesis installation by aisha kazembe  
parsons school of design, 2025

**live:** https://2110-rooms.netlify.app/

---

**2110** examines a single address — 2110 blue lawn road, iowa city, iowa — across the full arc of geological and human time. it moves backward through 375 million years of paleoclimate, indigenous land stewardship, colonial dispossession, and the american housing market, then projects forward to the year 2110 using climate modeling under the ipcc's ssp2-4.5 scenario.

the work asks: what does it mean to call somewhere home when the ground beneath it has always been restless?

---

## the rooms

**the past room** — archival text, audio, and ecological footage drawn from the land's pre-colonial and geological history.

**the present room** — a portrait of the house as it exists today.

**the future room** — a live climate model projected onto the wall, speculating on what the house and the idea of home might become by 2110.

---

## technical

- three.js equirectangular panorama viewer
- custom hotspot system with 3d sprites and html overlays
- plotly.js climate projection charts (ssp2-4.5, era5/cmip6 data)
- three.js 3d house impact model
- background audio: *perkins ln w*

built as a web installation. no framework dependencies beyond three.js and plotly.
